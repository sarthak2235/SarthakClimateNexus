Data-Driven Coupled Vegetation-Mammal Modeling Framework

2024–Present
  • Funding Agency: Center for Climate Physics, Institute for Basic Science, Busan, Republic of Korea
    This ongoing project develops a coupled vegetation-mammal model to simulate long-term ecosystem dynamics over 1,000 years. The model integrates vegetation fractions (grass, shrub, tree, and desert) with Net Primary Productivity (NPP), employing logistic growth, predation, and fire dynamics. NPP is estimated using the non-parametric ACE algorithm, while mammal interactions are modeled for over 2,000 species based on biomass, diet, and body mass. The framework enables closed-loop simulations capturing feedbacks between vegetation and fauna, providing insights into carbon cycling, biodiversity resilience, and ecological response to environmental change.

Climate Model Selection for Application-Ready Data in Fiji

2023–2024
  • Funding Agency: Government of Fiji, Climate Services for Agriculture (CSA), Australian Government, CSIRO, Australia
    This project focused on tailoring climate model outputs for the agriculture sector in Fiji. Using advanced evaluation metrics, 19 CMIP6 models were assessed for their ability to simulate seasonal rainfall and ENSO-driven variability. The six most skillful models were selected to provide localized, bias-corrected climate projections via a user-friendly web interface, with particular relevance for the sugarcane industry.

Unveiling Heatwave Impacts on Precipitation in Northern Australia's Summer Season

2023–2024
  • Funding Agency: Climate Services for Agriculture (CSA), Australian Government, CSIRO, Australia
    This study investigates the interaction between heatwaves and precipitation bursts as compound climate extremes (CCE) in Northern Australia, focusing on the pre- to early-monsoon (October-November) and late- to post-monsoon (March-April) seasons. Using column-integrated moisture budget analysis, it compares heatwave-associated bursts with independent bursts, revealing intensified peak rainfall, delayed maxima, and prolonged durations in CCE events due to sustained convective activity driven by cyclonic circulation. Vertical moisture advection, dominated by dynamic circulation, accounts for over 70% of moisture flux divergence, with heatwave-associated bursts showing enhanced convection from anomalously low mean sea level pressure (MSLP). The study also highlights higher precipitation magnitudes during both active and inactive Madden-Julian Oscillation (MJO) phases, emphasizing the need for improved forecasting to boost climate resilience in monsoonal regions.

Climate Dynamics and Agricultural Resilience in Indonesia

2023–2024
  • Funding Agency: Government of Indonesia, Climate Services for Agriculture (CSA), Australian Government, CSIRO, Australia
    This project explored rainfall and temperature variability across Indonesia’s diverse climate zones, using high-resolution CMIP6 model simulations. The study emphasized the roles of ENSO and the Indian Ocean Dipole in influencing seasonal rainfall, particularly during dry seasons. The findings contributed to climate-informed agriculture through integration with the CSA platform, supporting strategic planning and risk reduction.

Australian Summer Monsoon Energy Dynamics and Predictability

2020–2023
  • Funding Agency: ARC Centre of Excellence for Climate Extremes, ARC Discovery Project, Monash University
    This dual-study project investigated the Australian Summer Monsoon (ASM) through the lens of moist static energy (MSE) and gross moist stability (GMS) budgets. The first study compared reanalysis and AMIP-model simulations to evaluate how well climate models capture the ASM’s energy and moisture transport mechanisms. The second study analyzed the evolution and categorization of monsoon rainfall bursts using energy budgets, revealing distinct burst types and their controlling mechanisms. Together, these studies highlight the limitations of single-variable evaluations and offer novel insights for improving ASM simulations in current and future climate models.
